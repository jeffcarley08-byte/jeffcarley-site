The Climb Framework

Four Terrains. One Honest Assessment.

Leadership looks different at every altitude. The tools, challenges, and blind spots change completely as you climb. Find your terrain — then find your next move.

🌱
Terrain One
The Foothills — Early Leadership

You have the role but not the tools. The skills that got you promoted are suddenly less relevant. You’re leading by feel — and the gap between what you know and what you do is widest here.

New & Developing Leaders
🤷
Terrain Two
The Ridge — Developing Leader

You know what good leadership looks like. Doing it consistently under pressure is still the problem. The craft isn’t yet automatic — and pressure reveals the version of yourself you haven’t fully replaced yet.

Mid-Level Leaders
🏔
Terrain Three
High Altitude — Advanced Leader

You lead well. Your ceiling now is culture, not competence. Nobody’s telling you what your blind spots are — because nobody feels safe enough, or because nobody knows either.

Experienced Leaders
🌤
Terrain Four
Above Treeline — Senior Leader

You’re leading leaders. The metrics are slow and indirect. The question isn’t what you’re building — it’s what you’re building it through. Everything is exposed up here.

Senior & Strategic Leaders
