My Story

I Teach Leadership Because I’ve Lived It — On Both Sides of the Classroom

I enlisted in the U.S. Air Force in January 2008 and spent my first nine years as a Fuels Management airman — leading at the tip of the spear, where the work is loud, the stakes are real, and the textbook never quite fits the moment.

Then in 2017 I stepped into the classroom. For the next four years I taught at the Samuel O. Turner Airman Leadership School at Ellsworth AFB. Seven classes a year, six-week rotations, 240 hours of instruction per class — across four years. That’s where the 4,500+ hours come from. Not a guess. Not a round number. The receipts.

I was named the #1 USAF PME Instructor three years in a row — 2018, 2019, and 2020 — earned my Master Instructor (CIC 3) certification, held by only the top 4% of Air Force instructors, and personally trained five other instructors in how to teach leadership.

Here’s what I know: leadership is a craft, not a personality. It can be taught — but most of the way it’s currently taught is broken because it’s built for the classroom, not the climb.
